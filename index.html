Blockchain and Genomics: An Infrastructure for Secure Storage and Access of Genetic Data

Company: Magi's Lab Srl

Abstract

Genetics is a fundamental science, and the global volume of genetic data collected has reached several terabytes. This exponential growth presents significant challenges in storage, processing, and, most importantly, maintaining data integrity and usability. Blockchain technology has emerged as a reliable framework for ensuring traceability and secure data storage, especially when combined with distributed storage solutions like IPFS. This study introduces a novel blockchain-based infrastructure to enhance the storage, stability, traceability, and usability of genomic data. The system uses a multifaceted approach: data are vectorized, uploaded to a pinned IPFS architecture, tied to a multi-NFT structure on the Polygon testnet, and made accessible via a Web3 interface and a free utility token (MAG). A Solidity smart contract governs access and integrates with traditional Python-based genomics tools. This methodology has the potential to serve as a global reference model for genetic research.

The Challenge of Genomic Data

The massive amount of genomic data generated has outpaced traditional infrastructure capabilities, creating several critical challenges. This section highlights the key issues the proposed infrastructure aims to address.

Storage & Scalability

Storing massive datasets requires expensive infrastructure. Centralized databases are vulnerable to single points of failure, risking data loss.

Privacy & Security

Genomic data are highly sensitive and personal. Centralized systems face risks of data breaches, unauthorized access, and censorship, threatening individual privacy.

Integrity & Traceability

Ensuring data remain unaltered and maintaining a transparent, verifiable usage trail is complex in conventional systems.

Data Silos & Interoperability

Valuable datasets often remain isolated within institutional boundaries, hindering collaborative research and data aggregation.
